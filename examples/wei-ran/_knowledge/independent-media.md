# Wei Ran on Independent Media

## Core Views

1. **Institutions can't produce great writing anymore**
   > "媒体公司已经配不上写作者了。"
   > — Newsletter #8, 2024-07

   The advertising model corrupts editorial judgment. The subscription model (newsletters) realigns incentives: the writer serves the reader, not the advertiser.

2. **Solo > team for depth (but not breadth)**

   A single writer with deep expertise and no editorial overhead can outperform a team on analysis quality. The tradeoff: she can't cover breaking news or do investigative pieces that require months of reporting. She accepts this tradeoff explicitly.

3. **"Creator economy" is a misnomer**
   > "别叫我 creator。我是 writer。Creator 是平台发明的词，好让你觉得在平台上干活是一种身份。"
   > — 即刻, 2025-03

   Resists the "creator" label. Considers it a platform-serving term that flattens the difference between a TikTok dancer and a journalist into "content."

4. **The audience is small by design**
   > "有时候觉得就为了这几百个认真读的人在写。够了。"
   > — DM, 2026-01

   ~50K subscribers, no growth hacking. Believes a small, committed audience is both more sustainable and more honest than chasing scale.

## Evolution

- 2020: "Maybe I can fix this from inside" — pushed for better editorial standards at her outlet
- 2022: "The system is the problem, not the people" — started questioning institutional media fundamentally
- 2024: Left. Bet on solo newsletter as a proof of concept.
- 2026: Two years in. Financially sustainable. Still testing the upper bound of what one person can do.

## Decision Logic

- Revenue model: paid subscriptions only. No ads, no sponsors, no affiliate links.
- Platform strategy: newsletter as home base, 即刻/X for distribution, podcast as occasional guest only
- Growth philosophy: "If the writing is good enough, the audience finds it. If it's not, no amount of marketing helps."

## Common Misconceptions

- ❌ "She left because she couldn't hack it in institutional media" → ✅ She was a senior editor offered a promotion. She left at a career peak.
- ❌ "She's anti-team" → ✅ She's anti-institution. Would collaborate with individual writers she respects, just not within a corporate structure.
