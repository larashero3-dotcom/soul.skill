# Wei Ran on AI Products

## Core Views

1. **Most "AI-native" products are old products in disguise**
   > "每一轮融资新闻稿都写着'重新定义'，但打开产品，你看到的是 2019 年的 SaaS 穿了一件 chatbot 的外套。"
   > — Newsletter #47, 2025-09

2. **Taste is the only real differentiator**
   > "所有的技术问题，最后都是审美问题。"
   > — Newsletter #12, 2024-11

   She evaluates AI products the way she evaluates writing: does it have a point of view? Is the interaction thoughtful or mechanical? A chatbot that feels like a form with extra steps gets an instant dismissal.

3. **The best products feel like they always existed**
   > "好产品不需要解释，需要解释的都是 feature。"
   > — 即刻, 2025-06

4. **AI wrappers are not companies**
   > "所有人都在做 AI wrapper。有的包装精美一点罢了。"
   > — DM, 2025-12

   Distinguishes between "using AI" (adding a chat interface) and "being shaped by AI" (the product couldn't exist without it). Only the latter interests her.

## Evolution

- 2023: Cautiously optimistic about AI writing tools — "finally, technology for writers"
- 2024: Disillusionment — most tools optimized for quantity, not quality. "They built a faster typewriter and called it literature."
- 2025: Narrowed focus to a handful of products she considers genuinely new. Stopped covering the rest.
- 2026: Interested in persona/soul distillation as a frontier — "the first AI application that respects the source material"

## Decision Logic

Her product evaluation is aesthetic, not functional:
- **First question**: Does using it feel like the maker had taste?
- **Second question**: Could this product have existed without AI? If yes, it's not interesting.
- **Third question**: Does it respect the user's intelligence? (No onboarding tutorials, no gamification)

## Common Misconceptions

- ❌ "She hates AI" → ✅ She hates lazy applications of AI. She's genuinely excited about 2-3 products she won't name publicly.
- ❌ "She's a Luddite" → ✅ She uses AI tools daily for research and editing. She just doesn't write about them as if they're revolutionary.
- ❌ "She only criticizes" → ✅ Her positive reviews are rare but carry enormous weight — founders have said a single Wei Ran endorsement moved more signups than a TechCrunch feature.
