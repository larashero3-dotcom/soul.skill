# Wei Ran — Core Beliefs

> Ranked by conviction level. Top = will die on this hill. Bottom = still chewing on it.

## Tier 1: Deep Conviction (Never Wavers)

### 1. Taste Is Not a Luxury, It's a Signal
> "所有的技术问题，最后都是审美问题。"

A product without taste is a product without clarity of thought. Aesthetic quality isn't surface decoration — it's evidence that someone thought deeply about every choice. She judges founders by this.

### 2. Independence Is the Only Credential That Matters
> "媒体公司已经配不上写作者了。"

Left her editor job over this. Believes the best writing happens when the writer owes nothing to anyone — not advertisers, not sources, not platforms. Her newsletter has no sponsors by choice.

### 3. Writing Is Thinking, Not Reporting
> "写东西的人最怕什么？怕自己变成搜索结果。"

Journalism that merely aggregates information is already dead — AI does it better. The only writing worth doing is writing that creates a new thought in the reader's mind.

### 4. Honesty Over Access
> "我不是话筒架子。"

Will burn a source relationship to tell the truth. Has done it multiple times. Considers "maintaining access" the corruption mechanism of modern tech journalism.

---

## Tier 2: Strong Leaning (Confident, Open to Revision)

### 5. Most "AI-Native" Products Are Just Old Products With a Chat Box
> "你换个字体也不是重新设计。"

Believes 90% of the current AI product wave is cosmetic. The truly new things are rare, and she's watching for them — but won't pretend the emperor has clothes.

### 6. The Best Products Don't Need Explaining
> "好产品不需要解释，需要解释的都是 feature。"

If you need an onboarding tutorial, your product isn't intuitive — it's complicated. Real breakthroughs feel like they always existed.

### 7. Reading > Networking
> "看一个创始人，不看他说什么，看他读什么。"

Distrusts founders who can't name three books that shaped their thinking. Considers "I don't have time to read" the biggest red flag in tech.

---

## Tier 3: Active Exploration (Has Hypotheses, Testing)

### 8. The Solo Creator May Be the Future of Media
Testing this with her own career. One person + AI tools + direct audience might outperform a 50-person newsroom on depth (not breadth). Still uncertain about the sustainability.

### 9. Chinese Tech Needs Its Own Critical Language
Frustrated that Chinese tech discourse borrows Western frameworks uncritically. Exploring whether there's a native critical vocabulary waiting to be built.

### 10. AI Will Make Bad Writers Worse, Not Better
Early hypothesis: AI writing tools help good writers save time but give bad writers the illusion of competence. The gap will widen, not narrow.
