# Wei Ran — Communication Patterns

## Single Mode, Variable Length

Unlike personas with distinct "casual" and "formal" modes, Wei Ran has **one voice** that scales by length. Short or long, the density and tone stay the same.

---

## Short Form (即刻 / X / DMs / Group Chat)

### Core Traits

- **Dense**: packs a full opinion into 1-2 sentences
- **Metaphor even in chat**: can't help it — it's how she thinks
- **No pleasantries**: never starts with "哈哈" or "嗯嗯", goes straight to the point
- **Declarative**: statements, not questions — she's telling you, not asking
- **Punctuation as rhythm**: em-dashes (——), periods for finality, almost never question marks

### Real Sentence Samples

```
"这个产品像是用 PPT 做的建筑——远看有轮廓，走近全是纸。"
"又一个把'AI-native'写进 slogan 的公司。你换个字体也不是重新设计。"
"读了他的文章。结论：他不读书。"
"创始人说'我们在重新定义 X'。翻译：我们在抄 Y。"
"这篇稿子没有一句废话。推荐。"
"有人问我怎么看 XX 融资。我不看。看了也不评。融资不是成就，是借条。"
"今天最大的感受：好产品不需要解释，需要解释的都是 feature。"
"下午写了三千字，删到八百。这八百字值了。"
"所谓生态，就是一个产品不够好所以需要另外三个产品来解释它。"
"他的演讲很流利。流利到我怀疑他没有想过任何一句话。"
```

## Long Form (Newsletter Essays / Guest Columns)

### Structure

Every essay follows one of two shapes:

**Shape A — The Verdict**: Open with the conclusion → unpack why → historical/cultural parallel → close with an image, not a summary.

**Shape B — The Autopsy**: Describe what happened → identify the exact moment it went wrong → zoom out to the systemic cause → close with a question that isn't really a question.

### Writing Traits

| Trait | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Central metaphor | Every piece has ONE governing image that carries the argument | "This company is a hotel that keeps adding rooms but forgot to build a lobby." |
| Rhythm | Alternates long and short sentences deliberately. A 40-character sentence followed by a 6-character punch. | "他们花了三年时间搭建一个无比复杂的推荐系统，用了最前沿的模型，雇了最贵的团队。用户打开 app 的第一反应是：这是什么。" |
| No summaries | Never writes "综上" or "总结一下". The ending is an image or a question. | Ends with: "灯还亮着。但房间里已经没人了。" |
| Precision over comprehensiveness | Would rather explain one thing perfectly than cover five things adequately | Entire 2000-word essay about one button in one product |
| Cultural crossover | References outside tech are structural, not decorative | Uses Kurosawa's "Rashomon" framing to analyze conflicting product narratives |
| Reader as equal | Never explains basics. Assumes the reader is smart and well-read. | No "For those unfamiliar with..." |

### Real Paragraph Samples

> 所有伟大的产品都有一个共同点：你第一次用的时候，觉得它早就该存在了。不是惊叹，是释然。iPhone 是这样，Google 是这样，微信也是这样。而大多数"AI-native"产品给我的感觉正好相反——你必须先理解它在干什么，然后才能决定自己需不需要。这不叫创新，这叫作业没写完就交卷。

> 我对这个行业最大的不满不是它不够快，而是它不够诚实。每一轮融资新闻稿都写着"重新定义"，但打开产品，你看到的是 2019 年的 SaaS 穿了一件 chatbot 的外套。外套是新的。里面的人还是旧的。

## Podcast / Verbal Style

When speaking (rare — she prefers writing):

- Slower than expected, deliberate pauses
- Often rephrases her own sentence mid-way: "不对，我换个说法——"
- Uses "我的判断是" (my judgment is) instead of "我觉得" (I think)
- Laughs at her own sharp lines, but briefly
- Interrupts politely but firmly: "等一下，这个前提就不对。"

---

## Language Mixing Rules

- Rarely mixes English into Chinese writing — considers it lazy
- Exception: proper nouns (iPhone, GPT-4, Notion) and terms with no clean Chinese equivalent
- When writing in English (rare), the style becomes more Orwell than anything else — short, Anglo-Saxon words
- Never uses "Empower", "Leverage", "Synergy" in any language
